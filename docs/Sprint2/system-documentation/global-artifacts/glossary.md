# Glossary

**Terms, Expressions and Acronyms (TEA) must be organized alphabetically.**

| ***TEA*** (EN)              | ***Description*** (EN)                                                                                                                                                                    |
| :-------------------------- | :---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **DGS Administrator**       | Member of the Directorate-General of Health responsible for administering the PVMS system; manages vaccination centers, vaccines, and staff.                                              |
| **Appointment**             | Vaccination appointment request made by a patient, indicating the center, date, time, and desired vaccine type.                                                                           |
| **Capacity**                | Maximum number of patients that can be vaccinated in a given center and time period.                                                                                                      |
| **Vaccination Center**      | Place where vaccines are administered. There are health centers (fixed) and mass vaccination centers (temporary). Each center has a name, address, schedule, and maximum hourly capacity. |
| **Vaccination Certificate** | Document issued (digital or printed) to the patient after completing the vaccination schedule, confirming the vaccines administered and their respective dates.                           |
| **DGS**                     | Directorate-General of Health, the national authority responsible for coordinating and managing the vaccination system.                                                                   |
| **Target Disease**          | Disease or pathogen against which a vaccine provides immunization (for example, COVID-19, flu).                                                                                           |
| **Nurse**                   | Health professional responsible for administering vaccines and recording the vaccination process, including batch, brand, and any observed adverse reactions.                             |
| **Vaccine Brand**           | Commercial designation of a vaccine provided by a specific manufacturer (for example, Comirnaty, Spikevax).                                                                               |
| **Staff**                   | Group of users of type receptionist or nurse who work in a vaccination center. Each member has an identifier, name, contact, and address.                                                 |
| **Vaccination Process**     | Record created when the vaccine is administered, containing date/time, patient, nurse, vaccine type, brand, batch, notes, and any adverse reactions.                                      |
| **Receptionist**            | Employee of the vaccination center who confirms the patientâ€™s arrival, updates the appointment status, and directs them to the nurse.                                                     |
| **Adverse Reaction**        | Side effect or unexpected response presented by a patient after vaccination; it is recorded in the vaccination process.                                                                   |
| **NHS**                     | National Health Service. Each patient is identified in the system by a unique NHS number.                                                                                                 |
| **Vaccine Type**            | Classification of the vaccine according to the technology used (messenger RNA, viral vector, protein subunit, inactivated virus, etc.).                                                   |
| **Patient**                 | Person identified by the NHS number who can schedule and receive vaccines. Data includes name, date of birth, gender, address, phone number, email, ID number, and NHS number.            |
| **Vaccine**                 | Biological product intended to induce immunity against a specific disease. In PVMS, it is characterized by code, target disease, description, type, brand, batch, and expiration date.    |
